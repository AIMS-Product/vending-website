# Persona Review: Betty — Tech-Illiterate Grandparent

## Summary

- Pages reviewed: 6
- Issues found: 38
- Blockers: 3
- Overall gut feel: 2/5

I am Betty. I taught school for thirty years but I only ever use my iPad for
email and Facebook, and my grandson set those up for me. I read every single
word on the screen because I am terrified of pressing something I can't take
back. Looking at this "SEO Page Builder," my honest feeling is that this was
built for someone who already knows what all of it means — not for me. There
are lovely tidy boxes everywhere, which is calming, but the words inside them
are a foreign language. "Slug." "Canonical URL." "Noindex." "Funnel stage." I
don't know what any of that is, and nobody on the screen explains it to me.

## Blockers

- **No confirmation before Publish (Editor).** The blue "Publish" button makes my
  page visible to the entire internet, and there is nothing — no "Are you sure?",
  no preview-and-confirm step — between my finger and the whole world seeing my
  unfinished work. For someone like me who is terrified of irreversible mistakes,
  this stops me from using the editor at all.
- **The SEO settings panel is wall-to-wall jargon (Editor).** Slug, canonical URL,
  noindex, target keyword, topic cluster, funnel stage, lifecycle — none of it is
  explained, and I can't tell what I must fill in. I genuinely cannot complete a
  page because I don't understand what it's asking me.
- **The Redirect Manager is completely unintelligible (Redirects).** I don't know
  what a redirect is, the "301 permanent" choices sound irreversible, and there's
  no plain-English help anywhere. I could not use this page, and I'd be afraid of
  breaking the website if I tried.
