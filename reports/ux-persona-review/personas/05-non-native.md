# Persona Review — Yuki (05) Non-Native English Speaker

**Persona**: Yuki, 31, Japanese software engineer. Reads English well but translates mentally and stops on idioms, slang, abbreviations, and culturally specific references.

## Summary

- Pages reviewed: 13 (home, about, apply, contact, case-studies, news + 1 article preview, thank-you-for-applying, pre-call-resources, 404, legacy /blog 404, admin/pages, admin/news/new, admin/pages/new)
- Issues found: 20
- Blockers: 0
- Overall gut feel: **3 / 5** — Everything functions and the structural English is grammatical, but the site leans heavily on American idioms, slang testimonials, and one-word jargon, and the most important moment for me (did my application send?) is communicated in a tiny, vague phrase.

The site never breaks for me, but it repeatedly makes me stop and translate. The single recurring problem is **inconsistent naming for the same thing** (News/Blog, Step Inside/Apply Now, News/Blog CMS/New blog post) — for a mental translator, two words for one concept is more confusing than a hard word, because I assume the difference must be meaningful.

## Page-by-Page Review

Marketing English is mostly clear, but idioms ("increase your bottom line", "eliminate the guesswork"), the dual CTA labels ("STEP INSIDE" vs "APPLY NOW" both -> /apply), and the literal "0+" / "$0M+" placeholder counters create friction. Testimonials repeat the case-studies slang problem.

- Findings: 05-non-native-005, -014, -017, (-006 shared with case studies)

### /about — 3/5

Heading "How I Escaped The Rat Race" is a pure idiom. The rest is readable.

- Findings: 05-non-native-004

- Findings: 05-non-native-001, -007, -008, -020
