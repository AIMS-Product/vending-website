# Persona 14 — Mike, Competitor's User (33)

**Background**: Project manager. Two years on Webflow, plus WordPress+Yoast, Framer, and Wix. Evaluating this "SEO Page Builder" as a switch. Everything gets graded against tools I already know cold.

**The headline question**: Does this match or beat what I'm used to, and would switching cost me productivity?

**One-line verdict**: The editing surface is genuinely competitive — a live WYSIWYG canvas with inline editing and a categorized block picker that feels like Webflow's add-element panel, plus a readiness model Yoast wishes it had. But the operational plumbing every CMS has solved — publish flow, scheduling, redirect management, unsaved-changes protection — is either broken or below table stakes, and that's where I'd lose time every single day.

## Category-by-category findings

### Navigation & Flow

The admin shell (left sidebar: SEO pages / Blog and news / Media library / Settings) is conventional and learnable. The editor's top bar (Pages / Blocks / Save draft / Live preview / Share / SEO) maps cleanly onto a Webflow-style layout. Breadcrumb "Pages" back-link is present. No complaints on structure — the problems are in workflows, not wayfinding.

### Copy & Labels

The publish blockers are written in developer-internal language, not editor language. "Fix content 1 · Destination URL" is a serialized block reference, not a sentence a content person reads. Yoast/Webflow say "Your CTA needs a destination link."

### Forms & Input

Field types are mostly right, but the scheduled-publish datetime gives no timezone control and no post-save confirmation, and the redirect form clears all values on an invalid submit (exploration log: "values preserved: false").

### Visual & Layout

Strong. The live canvas, the block picker with per-variant visual previews, and the search-preview SERP snippet are all polished and match what a Webflow/Yoast user expects. CLS is 0 across every page. This is the part that would make me consider switching.

### Feedback & State

Mixed. "Draft saved. Autosaved 3:45 PM" is good. But the publish confirm card not dismissing after success, and the schedule field giving zero feedback, are state-handling misses competitors don't have.

### Trust & Safety

Destructive row actions (Archive, Duplicate) are confirm-gated — good. But two trust holes: no unsaved-changes guard when leaving the editor (typed title silently lost), and redirects can't be deleted (no way to fix a mistake). Both erode my confidence that the tool protects my work.

### Accessibility & Inclusion

Editor canvas has a `serious` axe violation (`aria-prohibited-attr` on the Hero/FAQ block labels). Several landmark-uniqueness violations on the new-page and revision routes. Keyboard tab order on the editor is actually clean (no tab traps), which is better than some competitors.

## Gut Feel — overall

**3/5.** As a Webflow/WordPress/Yoast veteran, the editing and preview experience is competitive-to-excellent and the readiness model is ahead of Yoast. But four operational defects — broken publish-readiness flow, broken-feeling scheduling, undeletable redirects, and no unsaved-changes protection — are all things my current tools solved years ago. I'd love the canvas; I would not switch until the plumbing matches what I already have, because day-to-day those gaps cost more time than the nicer editor saves.
