# Persona Review — Sam, First-Time Visitor (28)

**Lens**: I clicked in with zero context. I judge in seconds: do I understand what this
is, what it's for, and what to do first? If I have to read docs or guess at internal
jargon, I bail.

**Overall verdict**: The landing page (`/admin/pages`) and the create-page wizard make a
genuinely good first impression — I instantly understand "this is where SEO pages live"
and "Create page" is the obvious first move. But the moment I try to actually _publish_
my page, the product turns into a guessing game: it blocks me one cryptic reason at a
time ("Fix SEO title", "Fix content 1 · Cta Label", "Fix content 1 · Destination URL"),
and one required field isn't even on the screen it points me to. As a first-timer I'd be
stuck for several minutes and seriously consider giving up. The contradiction where the
chip says "Fix SEO title" while the SEO title field literally reads "Leave blank to use
page headline" is the single most trust-damaging moment.

## Per-Page Review

### /admin/pages — Pages list — Gut feel: 4

Strong first impression. The H1 "SEO pages" plus the subtitle and the four summary cards
(All / Drafts / Published / Archived) told me what this section is and its state in about
two seconds, and "Create page" is the obvious primary action. My one real first-timer
snag: the READINESS and STATUS columns are colored dots with no text label in the cell —
I can't tell what red vs amber vs blue _mean_ without hovering.

### /admin/pages/new — Create page wizard — Gut feel: 5

Best screen in the product for me. "What kind of page are you creating?" with four clearly
described cards, a 3-step progress bar, and a single "Continue" — I knew exactly what to do
with no prior knowledge. The reassurance "You can adjust details later in the builder"
lowered my anxiety about choosing wrong.

### /admin/pages/{id} — Page editor — Gut feel: 2

This is where I lose the thread. The split-screen (canvas left, big SEO/readiness panel
right) is a lot to absorb at once, and the path to actually publishing is gated by terse
internal-sounding blockers. The "Improve SEO title" / "Fix SEO title" chip contradicting
the SEO-title field's own placeholder ("Leave blank to use page headline") made me distrust
the guidance entirely.

### /admin/pages/{id}/revisions/{rev} — Revision preview — Gut feel: 3

Read-only render with a "Back to editor" link — fine and unsurprising. I only reached it
after publishing because revisions don't exist before that.

### /admin/pages/redirects — Redirects manager — Gut feel: 3

Clear purpose ("Send old page addresses to their new destinations") and a simple form. But
the table has no edit/delete control, so a typo'd redirect feels permanent, and the error
copy is jargon for a first-timer.

### /admin/pages/block-preview-audit — Block preview audit — Gut feel: 2

I have no idea why I'm here. "Compare every picker preview against the real resource-page
render using mocked block content" reads like a developer QA page that got left in the
admin surface. A first-timer who lands here would be confused about whether it's part of
their job.

### /resources/{slug} — Published public page — Gut feel: 4

Renders cleanly with hero, CTA, and FAQ. As the end result of my work it looks legit.

### /resources/preview/{token} — Draft preview — Gut feel: 4

Token preview rendered my draft without a login prompt — useful and unsurprising.
