# Persona Review — 09 Tom, "Just Get It Done" Pragmatist (50)

I run a small business. I'm in this tool because I have to put a page online, not
because I find it fun. I want the fewest clicks possible, then I want to get back
to work. I don't read paragraphs, I don't care about "governance," and the second
something hides the button I need or makes me hunt, I get annoyed.

## Per-Page Review

### /admin/pages (Pages list) — Gut feel: 4

Clean, scannable, the two things I'd want ("Create page", "Redirects") are top-right
where I expect them. Four summary tiles and seven workflow filters ("Needs review",
"Updating", "Needs links", "Metadata issues", "Schedule failed") are more slicing
than I'll ever use, but they don't get in my way.

### /admin/pages/new (Create page wizard) — Gut feel: 2

Three steps to start a blank page. I picked "SEO / Resource page," clicked Continue,
clicked Continue again, clicked "Start building." That's a lot of ceremony to reach an
empty editor.

### /admin/pages/{id} (Page editor) — Gut feel: 2

The actual editing canvas is buried between a left Blocks panel and a giant right-hand
SEO/readiness panel that scrolls forever (Readiness → Action Items → Search Preview →
Builder support → Revision history → Draft preview → Governance comments). The one
button I care about, Publish, is greyed out and the reason is in tiny text.

### /admin/pages/redirects (Redirects) — Gut feel: 3

Simple form, does the job. No delete/edit on the table is the catch.

### Revision preview / public page / draft preview — Gut feel: 4

These just render the page. Nothing in my way. Good.

## Summary

The tool is fine everywhere except the one thing I actually came to do: build a page
and put it live. That core journey is gated by blockers shown one at a time, a
required field hidden in a popup, a publish-confirm that doesn't close on success,
no "view it live" link, a schedule that blanks out with no confirmation and quotes
me US time, and forced wizard + tour steps before I can even start. If I were
evaluating this, I'd say it has good bones but the publish flow would have me on the
phone within ten minutes.
