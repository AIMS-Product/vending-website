# Persona 08 — Claire, Detail-Obsessed Perfectionist (37)

UX designer at a Fortune 500. I notice every pixel, every inconsistency, every
violation of design-system norms. I cannot unsee misalignment, mixed component
styles, or undesigned states. I am here to ask one question on every screen:
_does this look intentionally designed, or assembled from parts that never met?_

The honest answer for the SEO Page Builder is: the **individual screens are
genuinely well crafted** — the choice-gate wizard, the block picker, the empty
states are all designed with care. But the moment you put them side by side, the
system **falls apart on consistency**: three different primary-button colours, a
dark-themed card dropped into an all-light admin, an avatar that overlaps the
sidebar on _every single page_, an asymmetric block-selection ring, and a
publish flow that shows the same status in three contradictory phrasings and
leaves stale confirmation cards on screen. These are not edge cases. They are
the first thing I see.

## Summary for consolidation

The SEO Page Builder is, screen by screen, **better designed than most admin
tools I review** — and that is exactly why the systemic inconsistencies stand
out so badly. My top three, in order:

1. **08-perfectionist-003 / -004 (Critical)** — the publish flow contradicts
   itself: one state shown three ways, and a stale confirm card that survives
   its own success.
2. **08-perfectionist-002 (High)** — two primary-button colours (blue and
   black) across the same product.
3. **08-perfectionist-001 (High)** — the user avatar overlaps "Sign out" on
   every page.

Fix the button-colour token, the persistent confirm card, the avatar overlap,
and the dark-card orphan, and this jumps from a 2.5 to a 4.
