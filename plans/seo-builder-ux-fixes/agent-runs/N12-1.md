## Root Cause / Investigation

Pre-N12 the panel opened as a wall: SEO metadata fields + Advanced + Governance,
then the readiness panel — an info dump with no leading judgement, so a user
couldn't tell at a glance whether the page was publishable or what to do next
(I14). The blocker information existed (N1 checklist) but wasn't summarized into
a verdict. Fix: a verdict + fix-next leads, derived purely from the N1 checklist

- readiness warnings (single source of truth), and the settings dump is moved
  behind a Settings tab so Readiness leads.

A correctness risk found and handled: naive tabs would unmount the inactive
panel, dropping the Settings tab's form fields (schedule, governance,
uncontrolled defaultValue inputs) from save/publish — exactly the design
contract's "a collapsed drawer must not unmount required hidden values". The
tabs therefore keep all panels mounted and hide inactive ones with `hidden`.
